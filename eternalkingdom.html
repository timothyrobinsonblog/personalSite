Ganga Aarti at the Dashashwamedh Ghat in Varanasi, India. This ceremony is intended to be a display of devotion to the Ganges River, also known as Mother Ganga, which is considered sacred in Hinduism. Flowers, incense, chanting, and fire imbue the scene as young priests perform the ritual. The word aarti is derived from the Sanskrit word ArAtrika, which means ‘the light waved in darkness before an idol.’
